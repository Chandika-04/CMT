{% trans "POTENTIAL NEW MEMBER BILL OF RIGHTS" %}
Adopted by the Executive Council on December 20, 2021

Potential New Members of Theta Tau have the right to:

  1. An education. Theta Tau supports our student members' objective to receive an education from their host institution and will ensure that the new member education process does not impede that objective.
  2. Be treated with respect and as an individual.
  3. A potential new member education process that is free from mental, emotional or physical abuse or harm.
  4. A new member education process that does not violate the values of Theta Tau, or those of the host institution, or violate any local, state, or federal laws.
  5. A new member education process that respects and maintains members’ dignity.
  6. A new member education process that does not require you to participate in activities that violate host institution policies or local, state or federal law.
    7. Ask appropriate questions and receive true, objective answers.
  7. Make their own choices, free from the influence of others.
  8. Have and express opinions, without fear of retribution.
  9. Understand the financial, academic and operational commitments that they are making by joining Theta Tau.

This list should not be considered exhaustive.

EXAMPLES OF HAZING

We realize (and hope) that most of you have never been exposed to hazing before and may not have a clear understanding of what constitutes hazing. The following list contains examples of hazing our staff and volunteers have seen within the Greek Community.
This list should not be considered exhaustive.

  1. Verbal quizzes on the material from the pledge test.
  2. Requiring potential new members to memorize biographical information on the members of a chapter.
  3. Requiring, encouraging or coercing potential new members to do personal favors for initiated members.
  4. Involving pledges in the cleaning of the chapter house - potential new members should never be asked to clean a chapter house, or any other residence, even in cooperation with the initiated members.
  5. Requiring, encouraging or conditioning continued membership on the carrying of any item that they might not ordinarily carry, such as a pledge manual or other fraternity item.
  6. Requiring potential new members to address initiated members in a certain way or using certain language that goes beyond what a reasonable person would consider to be common courtesy.
